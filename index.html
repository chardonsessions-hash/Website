The opening myth

The Selkie Ceilidh

He found her seal-skin where the black rocks sleep and hid it where the rafters groan. By day, she wore his name. By night, the tide remembered hers.

The village thinks the ceilidh is only a dance: hands clasped, boots striking wood, fiddle lines rising like sparks. But every turn pulls the storm closer. Every chorus loosens the lie. The dance becomes a ritual, and the rhythm becomes a door.

This is the first Chardon Sessions lore file: a dark ocean-house song about captivity, longing, and the moment the sea takes back what was never truly lost.
